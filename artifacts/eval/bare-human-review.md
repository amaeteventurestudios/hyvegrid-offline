### Granite raw answer

2. **Frame Selection**: Only harvest frames with capped honey. Uncapped honey (raw) is more susceptible to fermentation and spoilage. Brood areas should be left undisturbed to ensure the colony's survival.

3. **Gentle Harvesting**: Use a bee-friendly hive tool to remove frames without disturbing the bees. Minimize the number of frames removed to reduce stress on the colony.

4. **Proper Storage**: Store harvested honey in airtight containers to prevent contamination and fermentation. Keep it in a cool, dark place to preserve quality.

5. **Colony Health Monitoring**: Regularly inspect the hive for signs of disease, pests, or poor nutrition. Address any issues promptly to maintain colony health.

6. **Nutritional Support**: Provide supplemental feedings, especially during periods of nectar scarcity, to ensure the colony's nutritional needs are met.

7. **Avoid Overharvesting**: Do not remove more than 20% of the honey supers in a single harvest to leave sufficient stores for the colony.

8. **Beeswax Preservation**: Leave some beeswax on the frames to help the bees re-capping the honey and to maintain the structural integrity of the hive.

9. **Educate and Communicate**: Keep the beekeeping community informed about harvesting practices to promote best practices and collective welfare.

By adhering to these guidelines, beekeepers can ensure that honey harvesting does not compromise the health of the colony or the quality of the honey.
