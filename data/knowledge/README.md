# HyveGrid Offline Apiculture Knowledge Seed

## Purpose

This folder is the first **public, challenge-safe** apiculture knowledge seed for
HyveGrid Offline. It contains short, plain-language notes to support field
reasoning for beekeepers and extension workers. It is data/notes only at this
stage.

This seed is intentionally limited to public apiculture knowledge. It avoids
private datasets, proprietary field intelligence, hardware plans, sensor IP,
partner strategy, and any internal operational material.

## Field use

The notes are written as cautious field checklists: what to look at, what to
check first, what to avoid doing immediately, and when to escalate to an
experienced beekeeper or extension officer. They are meant to be useful during a
real hive or site check, in plain language.

## Key checks

What this seed **is**:

- Public, challenge-safe apiculture guidance.
- Field triage, checklists, and next-step reasoning support.
- Written in cautious language (possible concern, check first, confirm by
  physical inspection).

What this seed **is not**:

- Not a certified disease diagnosis tool.
- Not a replacement for physical inspection or expert guidance.
- Not a complete apiculture reference.
- Not a source of private, proprietary, or partner-confidential material.

## Possible concern

HyveGrid is not a certified disease diagnosis tool. It provides field triage,
checklists, reasoning support, and next-step guidance. Any real concern should be
confirmed by physical inspection, and an experienced beekeeper or extension
officer should be consulted when needed.

## Check first

Before relying on any note, confirm the situation by physical inspection and use
your own judgment. The notes describe general, cautious practice; they do not
cover every local situation, rule, or crop.

## Avoid doing immediately

- Avoid treating these notes as certified diagnosis or official regulation.
- Avoid acting on a single sign without confirming by inspection.
- Avoid mixing this public seed with private or proprietary material.

## Suggested next step

This seed is intended for local SQLite FTS ingestion later, so the offline
assistant can retrieve relevant guidance during a field query. For now, the notes
are standalone markdown files only. Later steps (not in this task) will add
SQLite FTS retrieval, a prompt builder, a UI, and Yoruba support.

## When to escalate

Always consult an experienced beekeeper or extension officer for serious or
unclear situations: diseased-looking brood, a queenless or declining colony,
heavy pest pressure, suspected contamination, or unsafe site conditions.

## Files

- `README.md` — this file.
- `sources.md` — public-source categories and verification status.
- `hive_health.md` — colony strength, queen signs, brood, food, smell, pests.
- `site_readiness.md` — placement, drainage, wind, sun/shade, safety, water,
  forage capacity.
- `harvest_quality.md` — capped vs uncapped, moisture, food safety, storage,
  traceability.
- `forage_pollination.md` — diversity, flowering seasons, gaps, pesticide
  coordination, African/Nigerian examples.
- `hive_signals.md` — temperature, humidity, activity, clustering, water,
  ventilation; signals are not diagnosis.
- `glossary.md` — plain-language definitions of common terms.
