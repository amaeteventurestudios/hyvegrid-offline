# Site Readiness

## Purpose

This note gives cautious guidance for choosing and preparing a safe apiary site
for one or more hives. It is field triage and planning support only. HyveGrid is
not a certified diagnostic tool. Confirm conditions on the ground and consult an
experienced beekeeper or extension officer when needed.

## Field use

Use this note before placing new hives or when a current site seems to be
causing problems (flooding, wind, heat, poor access, pests, or pesticide
exposure). It is a checklist of things to look at on the land itself.

## Key checks

- **Hive placement:** Choose a level, stable spot with good airflow but not
  constant strong wind.
- **Drainage and flooding:** Avoid low spots that pool water or flood in heavy
  rain. Standing water around stands rots wood and stresses bees.
- **Wind exposure:** Avoid exposed hilltops or funnels between buildings where
  wind is constant. Wind makes foraging harder and can topple light hives.
- **Afternoon sun and shade:** Morning sun with some afternoon shade is usually
  helpful in hot climates. Avoid full all-day direct sun, which can overheat the
  hive, and avoid deep permanent shade, which can stay damp.
- **Stable hive stands:** Use sturdy stands that do not wobble or rot quickly.
  Raise hives off the ground to reduce ant, toad, and pest access and to keep the
  floor dry.
- **Access and security:** Make sure you can reach the site safely in wet and dry
  seasons, and that hives are protected from theft and large disturbance.
- **Safety around people and animals:** Keep hives away from homes, paths,
  schools, farm-worker areas, children, and livestock. Bees can defend a wide
  area when disturbed.
- **Pesticide neighbors:** Find out what neighboring farms spray and when. This
  is one of the highest real risks to a colony.
- **Water reliability:** Bees need a clean, reliable water source nearby all
  year. Plan clean backup water if the main source dries up.
- **Forage carrying capacity:** Check whether the surrounding flowers, trees, and
  crops can feed the number of hives you plan. More hives than the land can feed
  leads to weak colonies and poor harvests.

## Possible concern

Possible concern signs for a site include: frequent flooding or pooling, full
exposure to strong wind or all-day sun, deep permanent shade and damp, unstable
or rotting stands, hard wet-season access, hives near homes/paths/children/
livestock, known pesticide spraying nearby with no coordination, unreliable dry-
season water, or too many hives for the available forage.

## Check first

- Confirm by physical inspection: walk the site in both wet and dry conditions.
- Check the lowest point for standing water after rain.
- Check where afternoon sun and shade fall at the hottest time of day.
- Check stand stability and whether ants or pests can climb the legs.
- Ask neighboring farmers what they spray and at what times.
- Confirm the water source still has water late in the dry season.
- Estimate forage diversity and how many hives the area can realistically feed.

## Avoid doing immediately

- Avoid placing many hives at once on an untested site.
- Avoid placing hives close to homes, paths, children, or livestock.
- Avoid placing hives in flood-prone or permanently damp low spots.
- Avoid relying on a single unconfirmed water source.
- Avoid assuming nearby crops are safe just because they are green.

## Suggested next step

Place a small number of hives first and monitor colony strength, forage, water,
pest pressure, and any pesticide effects for a full season before expanding.
Keep simple site notes (drainage, sun/shade, wind, water, neighbors' spraying,
forage). If unsure of carrying capacity, expand slowly in stages.

## When to escalate

Consult an experienced beekeeper or extension officer when: the site has serious
drainage, wind, or safety problems, you cannot confirm whether neighbors spray,
the dry-season water source is unreliable, or you are unsure how many hives the
land can support. HyveGrid gives field triage and planning support only; it does
not certify any site as safe.
