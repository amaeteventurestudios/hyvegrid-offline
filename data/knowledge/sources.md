# Source Notes

## Purpose

This file records where the HyveGrid Offline public apiculture knowledge seed
draws its guidance from. It lists stable public-source **categories**, not
private field records, proprietary datasets, or partner-internal material. It
keeps the knowledge base challenge-safe.

## Field use

Use this file to understand the kind of public guidance behind each knowledge
note and to track what still needs verified citations. It is not a list of
invented facts or private intelligence.

## Key checks

The knowledge notes are based on these public-source categories:

- **Public beekeeping extension guidance:** General colony management, hive
  inspection, and pest-awareness guidance published by public extension bodies.
- **Public apiculture training manuals:** Introductory and intermediate
  beekeeping manuals intended for farmers and extension workers.
- **Public honey handling and food-safety guidance:** Public guidance on harvest,
  moisture risk, clean equipment, filtering, and storage for honey.
- **Public pesticide risk guidance for pollinators:** Public material on
  protecting bees from spray exposure and coordinating spray timing.
- **Public forage and pollination extension materials:** Public guidance on
  forage diversity, flowering seasons, dearth periods, and pollination basics.

## Possible concern

Possible concern: an exact citation or URL has not yet been verified for every
claim. Until verified, treat specific figures or strong claims as field-triage
guidance, not as cited fact. Confirm important points against an original public
source before relying on them.

## Check first

Before final submission, check each public-source category against an actual
verified public document and record the title and publisher. Confirm by reading
the original source rather than relying on memory or summaries.

## Avoid doing immediately

- Avoid inventing exact citations, authors, dates, or URLs.
- Avoid copying private, internal, or partner-confidential material.
- Avoid presenting any note as a certified standard or regulation.
- Avoid relying on a single unverified source for a strong claim.

## Suggested next step

For each public-source category above, identify one verified public document
(title, publisher, and stable public link) and add it here before final
submission. Keep the list to public, challenge-safe material only.

## When to escalate

Consult an experienced beekeeper or extension officer when a piece of guidance
seems important but cannot be matched to a verified public source. If unsure
whether a source is public or private, treat it as private and exclude it.
